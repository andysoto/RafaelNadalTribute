Nadal Throughout the Years

1986

Rafael was born on June 3rd, 1986 in Manacor, Mallorca, Spain.

1989

He started playing tennis at the age of 3, coached by his uncle, Tony.

1994

At age 8, he won an under-12 regional tennis championship.

1995

Tony encouraged Rafa to adopt a left-handed style, to give him an edge on the court.

2002

Nadal turned professional at age 15.

2003

At 17, he became the youngest male to reach the third round at Wimbledon since Boris Becker.

2005

At the age of 19, in 2005, Nadal won the French Open the first time he competed in the tournament, and his world ranking shot to #3.

2006

Despite enduring shoulder and foot injuries, Nadal won his second straight French Open and added four more titles in 2006.

2008

Nadal continued on in 2008, winning the French Open again, in addition to winning Wimbledon—where he beat rival Roger Federer in the longest final in Wimbledon history.

2010

In 2010, he was won the French Open and Wimbledon. His subsequent win at the U.S. Open made him the 2nd men's player to achieve the career Golden Slam—victories at all four majors, as well as Olympic gold.

2014

In June 2014, Nadal won his ninth French Open championship by topping Djokovic in four sets. It was his 14th Grand Slam title, tying him with Pete Sampras for second all-time behind the 17 won by Federer.

2017

After many injuries, Nadal won the 2017 French Open and the 2017 U.S. Open, returning him to number one. Nadal said of his comeback, “From the beginning of the season, it has been very emotional.”
